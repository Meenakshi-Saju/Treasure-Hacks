Cricket

what is cricket

Cricket is one of the world's oldest team sports. It originated as an unorganised game in the 1200s, with the first significant match taking place in the English county of Sussex in 1697. The popularity of modern cricket is largely owing to British colonialism, which extended the game to nations such as India, Pakistan, Australia, New Zealand, and South Africa, among others.

Format of Cricket

Test matches, one-day internationals (ODI), and Twenty20 are the three forms. Test matches are games that last four or five days, depending on the regulations that the sides are playing under. ODIs are 50-over matches played on a single day. Each side has 20 overs to score as many runs as they can in 20/20 cricket.Test cricket is the highest level which is played most prominently at the international level. The first test match was played between australia and england. Australia is the most sucessfull test cricketing nation with 47% wining percentage across their history.Later on, the other two forms were introduced. In 1971, there were ODIs, and in 2003, there were 20/20. Both were offered as a way to entice spectators who did not want to watch the game across several days

  7. Stumped - According to cricket regulations, a batsman can be given out if the wicketkeeper lays down his wicket while out of his crease and without attempting a run.

  8. Run Out - A batsman is out if no part of his bat or body touches the popping crease while the ball is in play and the wicket is fairly placed down by the fielding side, according to cricket rules.

  9. Hit Wicket - A batsman is out if he strikes his wicket with his bat or body after the bowler has entered his delivery

  10. Hit The Ball Twice - According to cricket rules, a batsman is out if he hits a ball twice other than to safeguard his wicket or with the consent of the opposition.
